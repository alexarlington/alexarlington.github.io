EQ

EQ, or emotional quotient/ emotional intelligence is the ability to observe, understand and manage our emotional self.

EQ and IQ are different as they interacts with different areas of life. Where EQ engages with emotionality, IQ, or intelligence quotient, engages cognitive skills such as logic, reasoning and analysis.

Individually, having a strong EQ helps us with:

  • Self-awareness - being conscious of our own feelings and actions
  • Regulating emotions, how we respond to our emotionally in a constructive way

EQ is important as it not only helps us with our own emotions, it also helps understand and connect to others.

Socially, having a strong EQ helps us with empathising with others, to understand another’s feelings. Once we understand our own emotionality, we can understand the emotions in others. This leads to connecting on a deeper more intimate level.

With this, EQ and empathy help us:

  • Being able to perceive emotions in others, including non-verbally.
  • Communicating effectively
  • Positive conflict resolution
